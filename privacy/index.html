Privacy Policy

NerveReset · Effective date: May 13, 2026

This Privacy Policy describes how the NerveReset iOS application (“NerveReset”, “the app”, “we”, “us”) handles your information. NerveReset keeps the content you create (quiz answers, mood ratings, exercise history) on your device. To run subscriptions, understand how the app is used, and measure marketing performance, a small set of anonymous events is sent to three trusted vendors — RevenueCat, PostHog, and AppsFlyer — detailed in Section 4 below.

1. Summary

  • The content you create in the app — quiz answers, mood ratings, exercise session history, streak, display name, preferences — is stored only on your device using Apple’s SwiftData framework and never transmitted to us.
  • To run subscriptions, measure how features are used, and measure which marketing channels brought you to NerveReset, the app sends a small set of anonymous events to three trusted vendors: RevenueCat (subscription validation), PostHog (product analytics), and AppsFlyer (mobile attribution). The exact data each receives is detailed in Section 4.
  • We do not collect your name, email, location, contacts, photos, health data, or other personally identifying information.
  • Subscription payments are processed by Apple through the App Store using StoreKit. We do not see your payment details.

2. Information stored on your device

The following information is stored locally in the app’s private sandbox on your iPhone or iPad and is never transmitted to us or to any third party:

  • Answers you provide to the in-app quiz and the resulting score tier.
  • Pre- and post-session mood ratings you choose.
  • Completed exercise sessions (exercise name, duration, date).
  • Daily check-in streak and related progress counters.
  • A display name, if you choose to enter one during onboarding.
  • Preferences such as the sound-cue on/off toggle.
  • A local flag indicating whether your subscription entitlement is active.

You can delete all of this information at any time by deleting the app from your device. Uninstalling the app removes the SwiftData store together with every setting listed above.

3. Information we do not collect

NerveReset does not collect any of the following:

  • Your name, email address, phone number, or physical address (unless you voluntarily contact us by email — see Section 9).
  • Your precise or approximate location.
  • Your contacts, photos, microphone, camera, health data, or motion data.
  • The content you create inside the app (your quiz answers, mood ratings, exercise notes, etc.) — that data stays on your device, encrypted at rest by iOS, and is never transmitted to us or to the vendors listed in Section 4.

4. Vendors that receive a small amount of data

To operate subscriptions, understand which features matter, and measure which marketing channels are working, NerveReset integrates three third-party SDKs. None of them receive your name, email, location, contacts, photos, or any health information. The data each one receives is listed below.

4.1 RevenueCat

RevenueCat validates your subscription receipt on its servers — this is what protects the app from common subscription-fraud techniques (clock manipulation, receipt replay). When you subscribe, restore a purchase, cancel, or your subscription renews, RevenueCat receives:

  • An anonymous identifier RevenueCat generates per app install (no Apple ID, no email, no name).
  • The product identifier you purchased (e.g. com.nervereset.annual).
  • The purchase / renewal timestamp and currency provided by Apple.
  • Country code as reported by Apple in the App Store receipt.

RevenueCat’s privacy practices are at revenuecat.com/privacy.

4.2 PostHog

PostHog is our product-analytics layer — we use it to know which screens people actually use, where they drop off, and which features matter. It receives:

  • An anonymous identifier PostHog generates per app install (a random UUID stored locally on your device — not your Apple ID, not your email).
  • A small set of explicitly-instrumented event names (such as paywall_viewed, exercise_completed, checkin_completed). Event names are listed in the app source code at github.com/Chamallox/Nervereset under AnalyticsService.swift.
  • Basic device context (model, OS version, app version) that Apple already exposes to any app — never the IDFA.

Events are sent to PostHog’s EU Cloud region for data-residency reasons. PostHog’s privacy practices are at posthog.com/privacy.

4.3 AppsFlyer

AppsFlyer measures which marketing channel led to your install — so we can know whether our TikTok / Instagram / search ads are working. Unlike PostHog and RevenueCat, AppsFlyer DOES use Apple’s IDFA identifier when, and only when, you grant App Tracking Transparency permission. If you do not grant it (or if you have not been asked yet), AppsFlyer falls back to Apple’s privacy-preserving SKAdNetwork framework — which sends Apple-aggregated, non-individually-attributable install data.

When you do grant ATT permission, AppsFlyer receives:

  • Your IDFA (an identifier you can reset at any time in Settings → Privacy & Security → Tracking).
  • The marketing channel and campaign that referred your install (e.g. “TikTok / spring2026 / video-3”).
  • Purchase events (so we can compute ROAS) — without your identity attached.

You can revoke ATT permission at any time in iOS Settings → Privacy & Security → Tracking → NerveReset → off. Doing so does not affect any other feature of the app. AppsFlyer’s privacy practices are at appsflyer.com/privacy-policy.

5. Subscriptions and payments

Subscriptions are processed by Apple through the App Store using the StoreKit framework. Apple handles your payment information, billing, and renewals under Apple’s own privacy policy, available at apple.com/legal/privacy.

The app receives from Apple only an anonymous entitlement signal indicating whether an active subscription exists. We do not receive your Apple ID, name, email, or payment method.

You can manage or cancel your subscription at any time in Settings → [your name] → Subscriptions on your Apple device.

6. System APIs used

In line with Apple’s privacy manifest requirements, NerveReset declares the following in its bundled PrivacyInfo.xcprivacy file:

  • Tracking: enabled (specifically: the AppsFlyer SDK may read the IDFA when you grant ATT permission — see Section 4.3).
  • Tracking domains: AppsFlyer ingestion endpoints (app.appsflyer.com, events.appsflyer.com, register.appsflyer.com, conversions.appsflyer.com, onelink.me).
  • Collected data types: Purchase History (via RevenueCat), Product Interaction (via PostHog), Device ID (via AppsFlyer, only when ATT-granted). All declared as not-linked-to-identity.
  • UserDefaults API access: declared with reason CA92.1, i.e. to read and write the app’s own settings (for example the sound-cue toggle).

7. Children’s privacy

NerveReset is not directed at children under 13. The app is rated 12+ on the App Store. Because no personal data is collected or transmitted, the app does not knowingly collect information from children. If you are a parent or guardian and believe your child has provided information to us, please contact us at the address in Section 9. As the app stores nothing off-device, simply deleting the app removes any information that may have been entered.

8. Your rights

Depending on your jurisdiction, you may have the right to access, correct, export, or delete personal data held about you (for example under the EU GDPR or the California CCPA). Because NerveReset holds no personal data on its servers — everything lives on your device — these rights are exercised directly within the app or by uninstalling it:

  • Access / export: the data you have entered is visible inside the app (Progress tab, check-in history).
  • Delete: delete the app from your device, or use iOS Settings → General → iPhone Storage → NerveReset → Delete App.

If you still wish to submit a formal request, you can write to the address in Section 9. Because we do not store a copy of your data, our response will typically confirm that we hold no information about you.
